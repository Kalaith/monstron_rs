# Hatchspire

Hatchspire is a compact monster-raising dungeon game about rebuilding a camp beneath a ruined tower. You begin with Pip, a loyal slime, a cold hatchery brazier, and just enough supplies to make the camp useful again.

Each day is a loop of preparation, risk, recovery, and growth:

1. Build up the camp.
2. Care for eggs and monsters.
3. Choose a party.
4. Enter the tower.
5. Fight, gather, and return before the run turns against you.
6. Spend the rewards on stronger buildings, better recovery, new monsters, and deeper expeditions.

## The Camp

The camp is the home base. It starts small, but each building gives the monsters and tower runs more structure.

- Player Tent: rest, save, and plan the next day.
- Hatchery: warm, care for, and hatch tower eggs.
- Stable: inspect monsters, manage the six-slot party, and track readiness.
- Breeding Grove: pair bonded monsters to prepare inherited eggs.
- Workshop: assign monsters to camp jobs for daily resource work.
- Shop: trade supplies and sell surplus materials.

Buildings matter because they turn tower salvage into long-term progress. A better camp means more ways to recover, prepare, hatch, breed, and keep expeditions moving.

## Monsters

Monsters are companions, workers, and fighters. Each one has a species, element, temperament, role, passive trait, town skill, bond, level, and condition.

Current species include:

- Slime: loyal water scouts that find small loot.
- Rootling: patient earth tanks that hold the front line.
- Glowmoth: curious fire supports that help locate eggs.
- Pebblepup: brave earth strikers with a knack for stone.
- Emberkit: restless fire strikers that help the workshop.
- Rillfin: gentle water supports that soothe injuries and help hatching.

Monsters are not just combat stats. Fatigue and injuries carry across runs, so the best party is often the one that is ready, rested, and suited to the next goal.

## Eggs And Breeding

Eggs come from the tower, hatchery recovery, and breeding. Hatching adds new monsters to the roster, while breeding prepares eggs with inherited traits from both parents.

Bred eggs can carry:

- Possible parent species.
- Element, temperament, passive, and town skill inheritance.
- Lineage quality based on parent bond and compatibility.
- Mutation chances tied to tower progress.
- A deterministic art profile for future curated monster visuals.

The goal is for each monster to feel like it came from a history, not a random roll.

## The Tower

The tower is the main source of eggs, materials, danger, and progress. Runs move through rooms with loot, eggs, enemies, exits, pressure, and boss encounters.

Combat uses a six-slot party:

- Front row: protects the back row and takes most direct pressure.
- Back row: safer placement for support and ranged roles.

Actions include basic attacks, role skills, defending, herbs, and fleeing. Victory grants rewards and experience; defeat returns the party to camp with penalties instead of permanent loss.

The current tower target is ten floors, with a boss encounter at the top.

## Daily Choices

Good days usually involve a clear priority:

- Need materials: scavenge, assign workers, or push tower loot rooms.
- Need a stronger roster: recover eggs, hatch eggs, or breed compatible monsters.
- Need tower progress: rest the party, choose front and back rows, then climb.
- Need stability: sleep, clear injuries, and let town jobs build momentum.

Every day should leave the camp stronger, the roster more interesting, or the next tower goal clearer.

## Controls

- Mouse: select buttons, buildings, trades, party choices, and facility actions.
- Enter: start or confirm the main available action on several screens.
- Escape: return to town or open the camp menu.
- Tower actions: explore, return, or continue depending on the current run.
- Combat actions: attack, skill, defend, use herbs, or flee.

The UI is still being tightened, so controls may move as the camp and facility screens become more focused.

## Current Direction

Hatchspire is playable as a work-in-progress. The current focus is deepening the monster systems, making camp actions easier to read, improving breeding outcomes, and preparing generated monster art for later in-game use.

The intended feel is a small but complete loop: raise strange tower-born monsters, make practical camp choices, take measured risks, and slowly turn a rough camp into a place worth protecting.
